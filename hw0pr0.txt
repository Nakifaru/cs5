It is fascinating to appreciate the influence of language on our thinking and having evidence in spatial communication. It would make an interesting study to document how quick the child dancer whose language uses cardinal directions picks up the directions in the new place and have an English speaker as a control. Is the sense of direction solely picked up in childhood or do adult learners of the language develop it too? How do the Guugu Yimithirr speakers cope when they learn a second language that uses direction relative to human position? The article evokes thought on the very foundation of effect of language on cognition.
